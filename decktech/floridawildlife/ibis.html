Pictures of Florida's Wild Animals

Ibis

     Ibis are quickly identifiable by their long curved beaks. These are used for picking bugs such as grubs out of grass, and this is where you'll most frequently find an Ibis. Or should I say "some" Ibises. They rarely are seen alone, they seem to live with an extended family, and you'll see the whole group rummaging through a lawn picking here and picking there every few seconds. There long beak makes it pretty easy for them, they barely even have to bend down! When the birds are young they are brown, but slowly molt, becoming the full white you see below. Despite their awkward appearance, they are good fliers, and with their wings extended they are much more colorful.


This was from a group of about 8 Ibises. Check out that beak. They are pretty nervous birds. Ever see the movie The Dark Crystal? They resemble the good wizards.

Commonality list

This is my list of the likelihood of seeing various creatures based on my experiences. Of course, you have to be in the right habitat, the odds of seeing a Dolphin in the forest are not very good. Also, these are based a lot on my local area of Hillsborough County (Tampa). A hyperlink means some kind of info and probably some photos are available.

Common

If you are in the appropriate habitat, you should run into one of these. In many cases, you can't avoid them.

  • Squirrel
  • Blue Jay
  • Pidgeon or Dove
  • Brown Anole (lizard)
  • Butterfly (various species)
  • Duck
  • Egret
  • Pelican
  • Seagull
  • Heron
  • Ibis
  • Small Crabs eg: Fiddler Crabs
  • Grasshopper
  • Snail
  • Frogs and Toads
  • Spider
  • Anhinga/Cormorant

Uncommon

These are harder to find. You'll have to spend some time outdoors and probably look a little for these. However, odds are you'll find one after a little effort.

  • Alligator
  • Turtle
  • Dolphin
  • Manatee
  • Cardinal
  • Snake
  • Armadillo
  • Stingray
  • Blue Tailed Skink
  • Horseshoe Crab
  • Raccoon
  • Possum
  • Ladybug

Rare

Difficult to find. Usually, it's just luck to find one, or you'll have to look very hard. You'll probably only see one every six months.

  • Green Anole (lizard)
  • Osprey (a fishing hawk)
  • Peacock
  • Boar
  • Spoonbill
  • Fox
  • Large Crab eg: Blue Crabs
  • Sea Turtle
  • Deer
  • Woodpecker
  • Vultures

Ultra Rare

Very difficult to find. Most people, even people who spend a fair amount of time outdoors, like me, never see one. Consider yourself truely priviledged if you do catch a glimpse of one in the wild.

  • Bald Eagle
  • Florida Panther
  • Bobcat
  • Otter
