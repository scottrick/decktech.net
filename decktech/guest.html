What is DeckTech?


DeckTech is a SW: CCG datashare.

What is a datashare?

A datashare is really just a fancy way of saying people sharing info with each other. I share what I know with you and you share what you know with me. This is how it looks in real terms

Okay, so how does it really work?

You submit a deck, article or tournament report and your submission gets stamped with the date you submitted it. The DeckTech computer system will then grant you access to our entire database of submissions for you to review, comment on, and rate if you so choose. The DeckTech database will be updated in real time, which means as soon as you submit your material, it will be available to the rest of the DeckTech members for review. The content on the site will change hourly. You can use your control panel to monitor the changes that interest you.

Your account will be granted member access for 3 weeks after your last submission date. If you let more than 3 weeks go by without submitting any new data, your account will automatically switch into guest mode. As a guest you will only be able to see the information that is 3 weeks old or older. Also guests are not able to review or comment on other people’s submissions.

So what is to prevent me from just copying someone else’s deck every two weeks, changing a card or two, and submitting it as my own just to get member access?

Nothing, except if your tech rating gets to low you automatically get sent back into guest mode and your account gets scheduled for review by a DeckTech Admin.

Okay, so what’s up with this tech rating you’re talking about?

Your submissions are available for review by the rest of the DeckTech members. They can rate your submission. Each time you get rated extremely low you get tech points taken away; anything rated medium to high and you get tech points added. If your submission looks like a copy and paste job you might not get away with it. Sure you can risk it, but you don’t want to get a reputation as a "corrupter".

Hmm, so tech points are given based on the quality of my submission?

That is correct. DeckTech members rate each other’s submissions. The points will be used to qualify you for awards. In addition as your tech rating goes up your opinion will count more, so the more effect your rating will have on others people submission. This is the way it works in real life, the expert’s opinions count more. Don’t worry though nobody will be able to rate you into guest mode himself or herself no matter how high their tech rating.
