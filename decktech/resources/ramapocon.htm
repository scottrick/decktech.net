Scott Lingrell, the mastermind behind last year's RamapoCon event, is back again this year with the bigger and better RamapoCon II, scheduled for May 5-7. Ramapo College in Mahwah, NJ will be hosting the event, and hotel information, directions, and ongoing updates are available at the official website- http://orion.ramapo.edu/~slingrel

The prizes to be given out are even more impressive than the schedule, if such a thing is possible. In addition to the trophy, the winner of the individual championship will take home a Darth Vader foil, and the individual who places highest in the team championship will receive a complete set of Endor foils. Other prizes for various events include Ben and Luke foils, a complete set of unlimited premiere, custom-designed RamapoCon t-shirts, and hundreds of rares, foils, and signed cards. The prize list is growing weekly as more donations and funding become available (with uncut sheets rumored to be a possibility), so expect even more by the time May rolls around.

Last but not least, who will you see when you come to RamapoCon? Last year's defending Champion Jonathan Chu, of course. Who else? Former world finalists TJ Holman, Ray Bordier, Clint Hays, Steve Brentson, and Matt Sokol, who also doubles as one of the two attending grand slam winners, joined by Charlie Herren. Other Masters in attendance include Chris and Brian Terwilliger, and Wojiech Jankowski. Rumor has it His Majesty himself, Juz Pakes, may attend. Team Albany will be bringing their trademark enthusiasm, with most of the Ghetto Squadron and the Philly Crew there as well. Ghetto Leader Greg Hodur will be overseeing Ghetto War with a dewrag, a golden spork, and a pack of Phillies blunts for the winner (and woe betide the unfortunate individual who comes in second... Just ask Chris Talbot...)

Last year's attendance number of 48 is expected to be left in the dust as more and more pre-registrations keep coming in. Scott Lingrell is accepting any questions, as well as pre-registrations through May 1st, so send your command card info to slingrel@ramapo.edu or crngwell@aol.com.
Admission for the entire weekend of events is just $7 if pre-registered by May 1st. Registration at the door costs $10 and will include all events. Everyone one will receive tons of prizes and special "storyline prizes" will also be given out all weekend. All serious SW players from the East coast and beyond will be there, so don't miss out on one of the best opportunities to rub elbows with some of the best and brightest there are!
