CommieBoy's Day of Destiny.

    Well, I suppose everyone is curious to know how the Florida Grand Slam went off. I'm up after just dropping off Matt Lush at the airport and driving most of the night, so here's a real unofficial(fun) tournament report.

    Let me start off by saying the amount of talent at this tournament was huge. I mean huge. The best example I can give is that I played all 6 games against players who were either just under or squarely in the 1900's and one 2000! Decktech regulars and swccg masters Matt 'Old Skooler' Lush and John 'Crash Beedo' Arendt had flown in to hang out with David and I and compete. Clint 'Gamemaster' Hays was there all the way from Tennessee. Gabe 'The Diplomat' Alonso was ready for his anticipated big comeback . Charlie 'CommieBoy' Herren had BUSED all the way down from Georgia. All the really good Florida regulars including Mike Bellefatto, Walter Cowart, David Irvine, Alfonso Martinez, Ozymandias, David Akers, Mike Fitzgerald, Lee Edwards, Nick Mayer(notable exception: Jason Burrows), and many others who I am probably forgetting due to lack of sleep. Really great level of play (and people). So after a really wild, off the wall, and dirty joke filled 2 and a half hour trip from Tampa in my 130,000 mile car jammed full with me(Neal 'dredlox' Razi), John, Matt, and Leonard 'Master Yoda' Mitchell, we were all ready.

    So let me cut to the chase. CommieBoy was on fire. It was, as John put it, "CommieBoy's day of destiny". He could do no wrong. With four games down, the only undefeateds were Charlie, John, and me. So it was going to be ugly. John and Charlie ended up squaring off, meaning they'd play each other twice, and I got 'lucky' (yeah right) and got to play 'only' Matt Lush and David Irvine (losing to both). Charlie actually took John down twice, a very difficult feat and ended up as the only undefeated (with a monster differencial). It was an amazing display of Star Wars: ccg by not only Charlie but nearly everyone.

    Just a few quick notes about the people involved. Let me start with the TD's. Chuck Lund and Mitch Velasco were great, funny, relaxed, and did an absolutely flawless job of running the tournament. Gabe was in hilarious form, as usual, he is a true entertainer(as well as a great player). After actually getting to talk to Clint, for those of you who don't know him but dislike him, he is really great guy. Ozy was a blast as usual. Matt's display of rapid fire, raunchy jokes impressed even me. John kept us all light with one liners. I actually really want to thank John and Matt for spending the big cash on airline tickets and coming down. And David of course is the ultimate straight man. I got to meet Leonard Mitchell, and 'Elminster' both really, really cool. Lee was another who just had me in tears with the comedy. Mike Bellefatto and I had an amazing game just BEATING each other down in space, and yucking it up all the way (battle damage spillovers three times and tallon rolls it was wild). And of course, good old Sorsceror, a great kid. Hell, even the store owner was cool! I could go on like this for hours, but I'll just stop with the people I most missed were Queen Jawa and Jason Burrows. I was really looking forward to seeing Jason again, he is a fantastic player, and a really cool guy.

    Finally, I want to end with Props to Commieboy. From what I could see he played incredibly. He was laughing, he was totally magnanimous in his wins, he even poked fun at himself. This is after busing all night from Georgia, meeting up with David in a strange downtown, and getting crammed into my afore mentioned 1986 Mirage Hatchback (a truely unsung hero to me, affectionately called the Falcon by all who know and love her), with SIX guys for a one hour trip to Brandon! So I think karmically, Charlie deserverd it. But don't let this victory go to your head, Charlie!!!

    So that's it for now, Lee and I did take some pictures, as soon as they are ready, I'll put some up here. So what was the point of this little report? Where were the decklists? Believe me, they'll be all coming with plenty of reports. My point here is for those newer or upcoming players to seriously think about traveling out to a couple major tournaments. As Gabe said, it's the only way to get good. And not only will your game improve immensely, you'll probably have a really difficult, but really fun trip, and walk away with some good friends. Thanks for reading.

Neal
