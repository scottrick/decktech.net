    Strategy
    Strategy:

    Greetings. I must warn you, this deck is a total pile. It is very fun to play though, and has surprisingly played well. I try to build decks around randomly selected cards, and this one was built around Trooper sabaac, which is why I have made some of the decisions I made. Hopefully, my strategy, explanation will be adequate, illuminating and entertaining!

    Think of this deck like TRM. It totally chokes, giving away only one force, the Holotable (which 9 out of 10 times I throw away to a force drain). Giving away absolutely no force can make any LS deck competitive! I then take advantage of the activation advantage and beat down where my opponent is weakest. Its simple mathematics!

    What I try to do with a deck is work side strategies into them. Sometimes they work, sometimes they fail! These side strategies are determined by a random card. In this case, Trooper Sabaac.

    So, I'm going to give a quick run-down of the strategies and tricks in this deck, then a brief description of matchups that I have experience with.

    I would like to get on my soapbox, and talk about piles. I almost always play a pile style strategy. It suits my playing style. Just to make it clear, you do not play the deck to set up these combos. You play the deck to win. If during the course of the game, a strategy makes itself available, and you have the opening you need to establish that combo, then take it. You don't play the deck to establish these tricks, you play the deck to win the game! Let the tricks come to you, don't fight to establish the tricks. This is a key point to SW:CCG. Flipping CCT does not win the game, forcing opponent to lose 60 cards does. That should be your primary goal.

    Starting Stuff:

    I start with An Unusual Amount of Fear, Home One War Room. My usual starting effects are Wokling (v), Strike Planning and Insurrection & Aim High. However, against direct damage decks, sometimes I forego Wokling and start YISYW, in order to pull HotJ.

    Opponents turn, take Rieekan and Mothma to hand using strike planning.

    Early Game Strategy:

    I'll deploy Rieekan to the Home One: War Room, and then use him to pull Echo War Room. Use Insurrection to pull the Hoth Docking Bay. Second turn, use Rieekan to pull Romas, deploy Romas to the war room, use romas to pull endor. Use insurrection to grab home one docking bay. I now have given myself 8 force, and nothing to the
    opponent. If I'm lucky, I'll have a Wesa in hand to grab the chambers. Wokling can pull Launching the Assualt, which can then pull Home One, which deploys for 7 and is power + 3. Move Romas up to the bridge, and deploy Mothma to the War Room, subtracting from destinies in space, and making my officers forfeit 6 machines.

    Mid Game Strategy:

    Disruption. This is where it gets absurd and my opponent just drops their jaw. Use Rieekan to get Sergent Hollis and Mothman to get a trooper officer. Play Lando Scoundrel. Get 3 battle destiny. If hollis sticks around, he can get the same rebel ambush until it gets grabbed. Trooper Sabaac as an annoyance, possibly drawing the shield (but I have yet to see it!) This deck is loaded with wild cards (weapons and rebel leaders), so you will win sabaac more often and not, and cause some nice direct damage. The rest of the power characters round out your ground force.

    In space, you will have the Home One that is ussually power 20 with zimH like text. It is rock solid, and it is draining at +1 where it is at!

    Your opponent will probably have picked their jaw off the floor long enough for you to pull another trick. When they deploy a power 2 or 3 character, laugh maniacally and noble sac Mothma or Rieekan and get back 8 or 6 force. Then, after they get up from falling out of their chair, place the Wokling out of play to get back Noble Sac, so you can do it again. The best thing about virtual cards is I see a million power 2 or 3 chumps being played now by DS.

    Late Game Strategy:

    You should have taken most of the locations off your opponent. They simply cannot keep up with your activation advantage. Sabaacing for some direct damage, and steady drain of 2 at Endor and the battleground site you have taken will slowly kill your opponent off. If they are still fighting, when the activate up and leave 3 force in reserve, for you know, weapon destines and battle destiny, play sabaac! Leave them with 1 to 0 force in the reserve!

    Tricks of the Trade:

    I'm going to skip this section for now, since most of the tricks have been mentioned above. Other then those tricks, its just the same old stuff. EPP hit and runs with resilence. Lando with anyone. You know the drill...

    Matchups:

    First, I will only speak to what I have played against.

    Invasion: Invasion does not deal well with chokes. In some cases, Thaneespi and Ackbar on Home One at the Naboo system is a game winner. So, I recommend go to the ground first, and then when you start to run thin, move to the system after opponent has contributed many resources to knock you off ground.

    Imperial Occupation (V): This has been a very bad matchup! I suspect TRM would be weak against IO as well. The direct damage that IO can cause by turn 3 or 4 hurts.

    It will be a tight loss. I have tried to tweak this deck to deal better with it, with cards like Mechanical Failure (pullable by Wokling (v)) and HotJ. Rebel Ambush has a nice bonus!

    CCT: Give them an officer. Rescue the officer. Once again, your activation advantage should be game winner.

    TDIGWATT: Free Executor sure does screw up your choke! But, Home One decked out against Executor Decked out with a few sense in your hand, I think you can outlast them to take back the system. HotJ will help against CCO. If you need to you can establish menace fades with Artoo in Red 5 and some chump piloting.

    Other matchups that I either played with previous incarnations of this deck OR theorize about.

    HDADTJ: This may be a pisser. This deck can choke you as well. With no battle grounds on the table, it means both players deal with visage. If opponent puts down a battleground site, you need to take it. Your tricks will take them by surprise, and you do have some serious power characters. EPP surgical strikes will help as well.

    Senate: You do have a lot of rebel leaders. I'm not saying I would invade the senate, but I would think about it if the opportunity presented itself. Not to mention Leia (v) putting Lott Dodd OOP.

    SYCFA (FWKTIL): Stop them fromm controlling systems with super home one.

    Watto: Weather the initial storm. Your character weapons should be able to take care of Watto. HotJ can help. Bluff opponent, save 2 force and then lose 2 force for them to put stuff back. Don't be predictable.

    Concluding thoughts:

    Thanks for reading. When this deck works, it works well! It has had some high differential wins. The losses it has been dealt have been close (both losses to IO,

    pre-tweaks). To play a pile, you have to play it confrontationally, and you have to battle early and take some risks. This deck is not recommended for high tourmanent

    play, but is a great warm up deck.

    Good luck, and keep playing the best game ever made!

    Justin 'Nitsuj' Stratton
