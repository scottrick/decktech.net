    Strategy
    Strategy:

    Greetings. I'm about to retire this deck, and I thought that I would share my experiences with it. For those of you that are not familiar with my deck designs, I typically choose a platform and run it for 3 months. During that 3 months, I'll incorporate subtle side strategies in order to experimate. I've been playing MKOS for some time now, and I must admit, it is incredibly strong. I would go as far to say that it is in the top 3 DS deck platforms available.

    Just a side point, pervious reviews I have received criticise that there are too many side strategies. I suppose this is my own fault, for not making it clear, you do not play the deck to set up these strategies. You play the deck to win. If during the course of the game, a strategy makes itself available, and you have the opening you need to establish that combo, then take it. You don't play the deck to establish these tricks, you play the deck to win the game! Let the tricks come to you, don't fight to establish the tricks. This is a key point to SW:CCG. Flipping CCT does not win the game, forcing opponent to lose 60 cards does. That should be your primary goal.

    Starting Stuff:

    Start MKOS, Tatooine:JP, Tatooine:DH (if you are not playing against AitC or Profit, if then start JP:LC instead of Tat:JP). Reveal Beedo as your rep. Starting effects are Power of the Hutt, Ket Maliss (v), and The Phantom Menace.

    Early Game Strategy:

    Work to get your non-unique Rodian in hand, so you can flip. Use Power of the Hutt and Ket Maliss to get the support characters to allow an early flip. Usually I end up with Might Jabba (pulled by AC gametext), Bib Fortuna, and Ephant Mon in the Chamber, a power character at another site (Vader, Sidious, Palpatine), and then drop the Rodian to a location to flip to either move with another group of chars, or as an initial beatdown crew. Hopefully, Maul will be one of your power char. After you flip (or before if opponent provided a non-tat. location), play WMAOP to get the Flagship bridge. Deploy Maul when you come accross him, and use TPM to deploy Nute Gunray to the flagship bridge. Now, your destroyer droids are destiny 7 when drawn for BD!

    Mid Game Strategy:

    Oo-ta Goo-ta Solo (v) and Beedos, Greedos, Feltipern Trevagg's are key. When you are drawing 2 BD's that are each + 2, you can do a lot of battle damage. I've had great luck dropping Trevagg to a locatoin, battling, using Oota to get the two destinies and then forcing opponent to lose 10 to 15 in attrition. The Destroyer Droids do some direct damage with their weapons, aiding the deck considerably. Play aggressive! You can deal out major beatdowns with this deck! Ket Maliss and PotH pulling extra supplemental characters is incredibly huge.

    You should be taking opponent's ground locations! Invade Gungun Tech's chambers with Garindan and Droids! Send Maul after jedi. You will be forcing opponent to forfeit key characters constantly, and you will be canceling destinies with Beedo, keeping your characters on the table. Did you search your deck using Ket Maliss and see a lot of 1's and 2's destinies? Use Bib during your draw phase to get those key characters to hand.

    Late Game Strategy:

    You will probably have committed most of your forces on the table. Start draining with the LightSabers, and try to squeeze out a beat down with what you have left in your deck. You should be tracking with impunity, drawing a masterful move destiny 8 regularly. In my experience late game for this deck is just finishing opponent off with piddly drains.

    Tricks of the Trade:

    Feltipern Trevagg:

    You can use Trevagg defensively, drop him at a location with lots of icons, to discourage opponent from battling. When opponent deploys and pays 3 to 4 force to battle, ride out the storm. Next turn drop a destroyer droid, and
    trevagg's text is turned off!

    Gunray/Flagship Bridge:

    Destiny 7 Destroy Droids when drawn for battle destiny. They also have a little extra power.

    Palpatine/Garrison:

    Key combo for late mid-game. These folks help drain opponent fast. Against Local Uprising (V), put them at the 4th marker! Opponent cannot ignore you, and with Destroyer Droids blasting speeders, you should win the battles.

    Insignificant Rebellion:

    Totally experimental. It occurred to me that I was winning 90% of the battles I get involved with, so I threw this card in to get to 60. It's alright, but admittably I don't use it much.

    Why no BF, BH?:

    He is a very powerful character, and I would like to fit him back in, but I took him out for BFiS1, to give me a little more space presence. But BF,BH adding 2 desinies, when those destinies are +2 each is huge. I leave it to you to decide, he was in earlier versions of the deck, but I squeezed him out of this version.

    Garindan:

    Destiny 4, cheap deploy for imps, free movement for imps. This text works when undercover. He's fun, and can be quite useful, if opponent drops 7 guys at one location and you can't crack it.

    Matchups:

    Watch Your Step;

    Your deck is bigtime built to stop WYS. Greedo, Oo-ta Goo-ta, Destroy Droidting smugglers left and right, this deck matches up incredibly well against WYS. Beedo canceling the one destiny they get with Oo-ta really works out
    quite well. Before the end of the game, you will occupy the cantina, and have kept the opponent off the ground. You will win the drain race, and can use Maul & Infiltrator in space to either cancel opponent's presence or cloak.

    LSC:

    When you battle opponent at the core, use Oota as your first action. Hopefully you will establish Sidious to POOP jedi.

    Profit:

    Get your free aliens (I recommend Mighty Jabba and Arica). Use PotH and Ket Maliss to get the support chars. Opponent will be unable to crack your chamber, and you can spread to other locations. Early flip will be quite easy.

    LU/L (V):

    I've only played this matchup once. What I did was take the 4th marker, and then let CHYBC do the rest of the work. You can try to move into Echo Base, but it can be tough. Palpatine/Garrison is huge in this matchup.

    Strong LS space (hidden base flip, EBO):

    Admittably, you will probably lose the damage race. Your ships can be somewhat strong, and you can throw forfeit fodder on them. If LS space is big in your meta, put Galid in the deck to drain faster on the ground.

    Concluding Thoughts:

    This deck is very strong against a LS deck with emphasis on the ground, or a LS deck that needs ground presence to set up in space (MBO, DBO). Its weakness is when opponent comes to you, taking out Jabba or Bib. Ephant Mon, Boelo,
    and Trevagg can help protect you. Thanks for reading...
