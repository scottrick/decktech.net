DeckTech Member FAQ
 

What does Passive mean and how do I become Active?

First things first, passive mode usually means you have either never submitted any decks, articles or tournament reports or it has been more than 3 weeks since your last submission.

To get out of Passive mode simply go to the submission wizard and submit something. Your membership will automatically be upgraded to Active and your submission added to the datashare.

Keep in mind only Active Members have access to the DataShare. If you let your account slide into passive mode you will only be able to view submissions that are older than 3 weeks. That means you will be getting everything 3 weeks later than the Active members. It pays to be an Active member.

Why can't I just type anything as my submission?

If you just type anything into your submission without putting any care or work into it, the Active Members might rate you into passive membership. Lets say you add a deck with 1 location and 59 Omni Boxes your tech rating could fall so low that you would be kicked back into passive mode. When this happens you have been pacified.

Here is the deal with Tech Ratings, everyone starts with 1500 when you submit each member can choose to review and rate your submission. If it is OK or better you get points added if it's bad you get points subtracted. Go below 1300 and your account gets put into passive mode for 4 weeks! OUCH!

This will insure both the quality of the reviews as well as submissions. ANYONE found deliberately abusing the rating system will be pacified.

What does pacified mean?

When you submit obviously 'corrupt' data into the datashare or your Tech Rating falls below 1300 you become Pacified. While pacified you can not access the member only Archive.

How are Tech Points awarded?

Points are added or subtracted from your tech Rating based on the average rating you receive for each submission. Each time someone reviews one of your submissions your tech rating is adjusted. Here is an example.

You submit your "Eloms Cheese" deck. Your friend, Bill "Vaderless" Smith, reviews your deck and says, "I give this deck a perfect rating ‘cause it always beats me".

Your rating jumps up 100 points! Perfect ratings always are worth +100 to your Tech Rating. The next person who reviews your deck does not share your friend’s enthusiasm, and gives your deck a rating of 6. What happens? We subtract 100 from your Tech Rating and add the new points (40) back to balance out your rating.

What is the Tech Points rating scale?

You are awarded points based on the average for each submission. The scale is as follows.

10: +100
9: +70
8: +40
7: +20
6: +10
5: 0
4: -10
3: -30
2: -70
1: -100

In addition for each submission you review your rating will increase by 1 point.

Members with higher rating will gain special privileges that will distinguish them when they submit or post on our new Members Only Message Board now under development.
