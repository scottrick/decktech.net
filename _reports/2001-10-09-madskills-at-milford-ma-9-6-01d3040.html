Thank you Columbus for rediscovering a little island that gave you credit for 2 whole continents. Seriously what an overrated holiday, Columbus Day. Why don’t we have Ben Franklin day because he discovered electricity. At least we use electricity a lot in our daily lives now; we don’t need to worry about the colonization of a new continent anymore. Oh I’m ranting.

So there was another Milford Tournament this month, just like there is every month. Wow. My friends Rich Hinds and Matt McGann decided to take a break from the game so they can play Raw Deal, so I had to work things out with my padawan Jon "Wow" Bragg to find a ride to the tournament. Well things were looking grim, and we finally decided to bribe Rich Hinds to give us a ride even though he wasn’t going (pay for his gas and give him a few bucks) but Jon’s father didn’t like that and gave us a ride anyways. Cool.

So since it was up in the air whether we were going or not, we didn’t playtest much so I just whipped 2 decks together and helped Jon build 2. I was using WYS and Dark Surgeon and built Jon a Watto (which i should have used) and he built himself a LS Senate after trying 9872149874932712349 decks to beat DS Senate (well LS Senate didn&#8217;t even work that well either but might as well try).

So we get to the tournament about an hour and a half early and the store isn’t even open. We are walking in downtown ghetto Milford and go to the busiest Dunkin’ Donuts ever and got some breakfast. Now I’ll explain the layout of downtown Milford. It seems there are only 3 things they have for sale in Milford, jewelry, signs, and places to make signs and laminate them. We past Milford Sign, Milford Sign Shop, Milford Sign Maker, Milford Sign Place, Milford Sign Laminator on this trip. Why they need so many signs you might ask? Because the town is so ghetto they are always getting stolen I guess. Then we past by a store called "Moron’s Jewelry", good old Milford.

Then we came across this old boarded up store that had the biggest spider-web on it I’ve ever seen with the biggest spider on it. But the spider was dead, and then an even bigger Spider came down to eat it, it was pretty cool. So then in the distance we saw the card store, a shining star in the sky of beat to hell stores of Milford. We got there and it wasn’t open yet, so we had to keep walking around. Jon saw the music store next door, and we walked in expecting it to be ghetto like the rest of Milford. Alas to our surprise we walk in to the biggest music store I’ve ever seen in my life. After walking around I came across the biggest case of Harmonicas ever. What a great idea, try out Harmonicas to piss the music employees off. Well I expected just to goof off with these for like a half hour until the card store opened and then just pretend I didn&#8217;t have the 5 bucks for one. However, after playing one, I was hooked. Can you believe they have Harmonicas that cost 100+ dollars? Hmmm, maybe in 6 months when Star Wars is dead???? Oh I’m rambling again. Well yeah I bought one and started figuring out some tunes and we went into the card store which was finally open.

We still had a little less than an hour to spare so I decided to look through their box of commons and uncommons. I found a few and went to buy them and the woman was like "they will be a buck each". Boy I wish I could get away with selling my uncommons for a buck each. Christ I’d like to sell rares for that much. I convinced the woman to let me have them both for a buck because I needed them that bad, and they would never sell SWCCG again in like 6 months. Well then Steve Brentson came in and asked me if I wanted to play a game, and I said sure, might as well get used to playing again.

So he pulls out Watto (which I played against at the last tournament) and I got my WYS deck. I’m not going into details here, let me just say it was good that I got that game out of my system because I sucked. To top it all off, my horrible luck was with me too. Hope this isn’t a sign of things to come.

Well people start showing up finally and we are about to get under way, some of the notables were Eric Hunter, Justin Desai, and Brentson. Notably missing: Baroni, Scheimer.

Game 1

My LS vs. Joshua Loneau (1500) YMSYL

So I get a chance to redeem myself with the WYS deck. He starts with the marker site and uses twi-lek for YMSYL because he hasn’t bought much ds2 yet (he was making a comeback in the game he said). So I start Battle Plan/DTF, Insurrection/Aim High and Squadron Assignments. His turn he draws and says go. I start fast flipping 2nd turn. He gets out a walker and starts dealing direct damage while I am draining unchallenged all over Tatooine. I realized he wasn’t going to fight me so I just held back on my sites. I lost Menace Fades (doh) and he got off a walker garrison drain. Thank god for control tunnel visions. In the end I showed him how to track when I retrieved 19 with my edges.

Fw 2 +23?

Highs: Redeeming my WYS.
Lows: Losing Menace Fades.

Game 2

My DS vs Johnny Vegas (1550s) QMC

Nice. I get to play mista Johnnnnnnnnnnnnnny Vegas! He pulls out QMC and I pull out my AOBS and we both say at the same time "This is gonna be a good game". Indeed it was. I did not expect QMC in the meta, as this is a tough deck for Black Sun needing to kill Luke and their lack of space power. I dealt damage unchallenged in the beginning, but soon he got his drains and retrieval going with Cloud City Celebration. It is a pain in the @#$% killing Luke against QMC because of Path Of Least Resistance. Needless to say, I didn’t kill Luke until Johnny put him in front of my drain out of necessity. That proved to be the end, as we were both hurt by battle plan (he only had space, I only had ground) but I could deal direct damage of my objective. Wow, great game Johnny.

FW +4

4 +27?

Highs: An incredible game.
Lows: None it was an incredible game. Killing Luke would have been nice.
